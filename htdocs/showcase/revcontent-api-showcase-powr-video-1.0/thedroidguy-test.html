Amazon Cloud Cam vs Arlo vs Nest Comparison of Best Wireless Home Security Cameras in 2018

by Valerie November 8, 2017, 1:34 am

Home security systems are used in abundance today, mainly for the kind of utility they offer. These cameras can let you keep an eye on your home even if you’re thousands of miles away, assuring you of the safety of your house or office space even if you’re not physically present in the location. The fact that you can check the cameras directly from your smartphone is doubly helpful and convenient. We’re going to talk about a handful of home security cameras today, letting you pick the best one suited for your home or office. Not all cameras are the same, and there will be some obvious advantages and disadvantages to each offering. Keeping this in mind, it’s important to know what exactly you’re looking for before you get a camera for your home.

Amazon Cloud Cam

Features

Freshly announced in the market, Amazon Cloud Cam is touted as a competitor to Nest Cam and a handful of other offerings available in the market. It is based heavily on the cloud, and the users will be required to make subscriptions to record motion detection clips for prolonged intervals. The Cloud Cam can be bought in bundles as well, much like every other home security camera available in the market today. It is a pretty nifty product overall, although I personally like the fact that it can record two way audio. Since this is an Amazon product, Alexa integration is included by default. This will let you interact with the folks at home, and even ask your dog to stop barking, all using the power of Alexa. It’s a pretty nifty product, and something that’s going to be discussed heavily in the days to come. The Cloud Cam records video in 1080p, and also supports night vision, allowing you to get a perfectly good look of your home or office. Motion detection should work just as expected here, although we’re yet to encounter demonstrations of the device since it’s only been announced as of now.

Pricing

The Cloud Cam will cost $119.99 for one unit, and you can get 2 for $199.99 or 3 for $289.99, so there are obvious incentives to getting three cameras at once. Since they can all pair with each other, it makes sense to have cameras around key areas of your house. As for the cloud storage services, Amazon offers free access to 24 hours of footage of up to 3 cameras. The paid tiers start from $6.99 per month or $69 per year, which gives you access to 7 days worth of footage from up to 3 connected cameras. The Extended tier will set you back by $9.99 per month or $99 per year and will offer 14 days worth of footage from up to 5 cameras. The Pro tier will set you back by $19.99 per month or $199.99 per year, and will give you access to 30 days of motion detection footage from up to 10 cameras. The Pro tier is suited for an office like environment with large areas to monitor. You will have to wait until November 8 to get the Amazon Cloud Cam, though.

Netgear Arlo

Features

The Netgear Arlo is not as fancy as the Amazon Cloud Camera that we talked about above, but is still a perfectly decent camera for indoor or outdoor use. It works on CR123 lithium batteries, which means you will be required to replace them whenever they run out. They efficiency of the cameras will depend heavily on the proximity to the Netgear Arlo hub included in some units. It records videos in 720p, and there’s support for night vision mode here as well. Luckily for the customers, Netgear offers all the mounting equipment (including magnetic mounts) with the cameras, so you’re not required to get them separately. Just unbox the packaging and you will find everything that you need right there. The cameras can sustain weather of any kind, so the durability is a special feature with the Netgear Arlo. Naturally, the footage can be seen using the dedicated Arlo app for your smartphone or tablet. It’s pretty much like any other home security camera in that regard, although there are a handful of features that set it apart from the competition.

One primary complaint I have with the Arlo is that it doesn’t record audio. This means that you will have access to video recordings sans the audio. This could be a deal breaker for a few customers given how most modern day security cameras can record two way audio.

Pricing

The camera itself will set you back by $148.99. Video recording and storage might require you to shell out some money, depending on the kind of usage you have. The free tier offers 7 days worth of recordings from up to 5 cameras. Netgear offers a 30 day recording option for up to 10 cameras for $10 per month, and a $15 per month option which offers 60 days worth of video footage along with support for 15 cameras. You will require a separate hub if you’re going to use 15 cameras, however. While the subscription costs are minimal, the Arlo will cost you a small fortune if you’re adding cameras ($149 per unit).

Nest Cam Indoor

Features

This is more of a webcam than a standalone camera, making it suitable for getting a live stream of what’s happening at your home. It doesn’t offer large storage options, so you will be limited to picking 10 or 30 days worth of recordings. It can record videos in 1080p, with support for night vision enabled by default. As far as cameras are concerned, the Nest Cam Indoor is a pretty decent option and highly recommended for those who want a snappy and functional camera for their homes or offices.

Pricing

The camera itself will set you back by $198, with the 10 day cloud recording option costing $10 per month, while the 30 day recording option will set you back by $30. There aren’t a lot of options are far as storage tiers are concerned, which is perhaps the only setback here.

Verdict

As far as home security cameras go, it seems like the Amazon Cloud Cam is the best choice right now. Not only for features like Alexa assistant, but also for the inexpensive cloud storage options. The only negative here is that the customers will have to wait until the 8th of November to get the product. If you don’t mind the wait, we strongly recommend the Amazon Cloud Cam.
