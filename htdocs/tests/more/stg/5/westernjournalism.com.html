Trump Delays Signing Executive Order On Voter Fraud

"... he got jammed up ..."

by Charles Campbell January 26, 2017 at 5:54pm

Share on FacebookShare Tweet Email Email Print

Advertisement - story continues below

President Donald Trump will be signing an executive action to begin an investigation into voter fraud Friday or Saturday, delaying a move that was expected to be completed by Thursday.

White House Press Secretary Sean Spicer told reporters late Thursday that Trump was running behind on a few matters due to the Republican leadership retreat in Philadelphia.

Trump arrived back at the White House 25 minutes later than expected.

“The president got back a little late and he got jammed up on some meetings that needed to occur,” Spicer said. Were going to roll all [of the executive-order signings] into Friday and Saturday.

During the flight to the summit, Spicer had said Trump would sign a document that “will be a follow-up on the announcement yesterday of his commitment to better understand voter fraud.”

On Monday night, Trump told members of Congress that 3 million to 5 million illegal voters were the reason Democratic rival Hillary Clinton won the popular vote in November.

Advertisement - story continues below

The president followed up with a tweet Wednesday saying he would be ordering a major investigation into America’s voting system.

I will be asking for a major investigation into VOTER FRAUD, including those registered to vote in two states, those who are illegal and….

— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) January 25, 2017

even, those registered to vote who are dead (and many for a long time). Depending on results, we will strengthen up voting procedures!

— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) January 25, 2017

Later that day, Trump said in an interview with ABC News’ David Muir that his administration had “a lot to look into.”

Muir said the president’s allegations about voter fraud were unfounded and that there was no evidence to support them.

“There are millions of votes, in my opinion,” Trump said, citing a Pew report.

“Were going to launch an investigation to find out. And then the next time — and I will say this, of the votes cast, none of them come to me,” he added. “They would all be for the other side. None of them come to me. But when you look at the people that are registered, dead, illegal, and two states, in some cases, maybe three states.”
